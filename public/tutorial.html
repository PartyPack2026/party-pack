Couch Pack
How to Play
Getting Started
1

Open the Host Screen

One person opens couchpack.one on a TV, laptop, or big screen. This is the shared display everyone watches.

2

Players Join on Their Phones

Everyone else goes to couchpack.one/join or scans the QR code. Pick an avatar and enter your name.

3

Host Picks a Game

Once everyone's joined, the host selects a game from the strip and hits Start Game. Players do everything on their phones — the big screen shows the action.

4

React & Have Fun

During any game, tap the reaction buttons at the bottom of your screen. Your reaction flies across the big screen for everyone to see!

The Games
Mind Meld 2+
5–8 min
›
A question appears on screen. Everyone types the first answer that comes to mind. Match someone else's answer exactly and you both score big — a perfect Mind Meld!
1
A question appears — type the first thing that pops into your head
2
Answers are revealed — matches score points, unique answers score nothing
3
If everyone says the same thing — massive group bonus!
Pro Tip
Think like the group, not like yourself. The obvious answer is usually the right call.
Hot Take 2+
5–7 min
›
A bold statement appears. Tap HOT TAKE if you think it's controversial, or Normal Take if you think most people agree. Points depend on who voted with you — and against you.
1
Read the statement and vote Hot Take or Normal Take
2
Safe majority = +50. Bold minority = +150. Lone dissenter = -100!
3
Perfect split = everyone gets chaos points!
Pro Tip
Sometimes the safe play wins. Sometimes being the lone rebel pays off. Read the room.
Voltage 2+
6–8 min
›
A category appears — type one thing that fits. Unique answers score big. Shared answers score less. If everyone says the same thing, it's barely worth anything. Speed helps!
1
See the category, type your best answer before time runs out
2
Unique answer = +300 pts. Shared = +150. Everyone says it = +50
3
No answer = 0 pts
Pro Tip
Go weird. Obvious answers are almost always shared. The weirder the better.
The Mole 4+
8–12 min
›
Most players get a secret word they must get someone else to say naturally in conversation. One player is The Mole — their job is to catch the others before they succeed.
1
Check your phone — you'll get a secret word and hints, or be told you're The Mole
2
Talk naturally in the group and try to slip your word into conversation
3
The Mole watches for suspicious phrases and tries to catch players
4
After time's up, everyone votes on who they think The Mole is
Pro Tip
Don't be too obvious. If your word is "umbrella", don't say "speaking of rain..."
Psychic 3+
8–12 min
›
One player is The Psychic. They know a secret word. Everyone else asks yes/no questions — but The Psychic can ONLY answer using a set of random nonsense words. First to guess the word wins!
1
The Psychic sees their secret word and 5 nonsense words to use as answers
2
Guessers ask yes/no questions — the Psychic picks whichever nonsense word feels right
3
Wrong guess = -100 pts. Correct guess = +500 pts for the guesser and +300 for the Psychic
Pro Tip
Psychics: try to use positive-sounding nonsense for yes, negative-sounding for no. Guessers: listen for the vibe, not the word.
Copycat 3+
8–12 min
›
Like the telephone game but with drawings! The first player draws a prompt. The next player copies that drawing — without seeing the original prompt. Chaos ensues. Vote for the best result!
1
Player 1 gets the original prompt and draws it
2
Player 2 sees only Player 1's drawing and copies it as best they can
3
This continues until everyone has drawn — then the full chain is revealed
4
Everyone votes for their favourite. Most votes = most points
Pro Tip
The drawings always get funnier the further down the chain. Embrace the chaos.
Quiplash 2+
8–12 min
›
Everyone gets a funny fill-in-the-blank prompt. Write the funniest answer you can. Then answers go head-to-head and everyone votes for their favourite. Even you get to vote!
1
Read your prompt and type your funniest answer — 60 seconds!
2
Two answers go head-to-head on the big screen
3
Everyone votes — including the two competitors (just not for themselves)
Pro Tip
Specific is funnier than vague. "A confused penguin in a job interview" beats "something weird".
Fibbage 2+
8–10 min
›
A bizarre true fact appears with a blank. Everyone writes a convincing fake answer. Then everyone votes on which one they think is real. Fool others with your lie, or find the truth!
1
Read the fact prompt and write a convincing lie to fill the blank
2
All lies + the real answer are shown — vote for what you think is true
3
Find the truth = +500. Fool others with your lie = +400 per person fooled
Pro Tip
The truth is usually weirder than you'd expect. If an answer sounds too strange to be made up, it might be real.
Drawful 2+
8–12 min
›
Take turns drawing a weird prompt on your phone while everyone else watches live and tries to guess what it is. Wrong guesses cost points. Right guesses score big for both guesser and drawer!
1
When it's your turn, you get a prompt — draw it as best you can
2
Everyone else watches your drawing appear live on their screen
3
Guess correctly = +1000. Fool others with a fake guess = +500 each
Pro Tip
Don't try to be a great artist. Clear, simple drawings are easier to guess than detailed masterpieces.
Trivia Knockout 2+
6–10 min
›
Multiple choice trivia with a brutal twist — get a question wrong and you're eliminated. Answer correctly and build a streak for bonus points. Last player standing wins!
1
A question appears — tap your answer as fast as you can
2
Wrong answer = eliminated. Correct = +300 pts + streak bonus
3
Eliminated? Watch and cheer (or jeer) from the sidelines
Pro Tip
Streaks give big bonuses. If you're on a roll, don't rush — accuracy beats speed here.
Poll Mine 2+
6–8 min
›
Vote on a fun question, then try to predict how the whole group answered. The closer your ranking matches reality, the more points you score. It's all about reading your friends!
1
A question appears — vote for your answer
2
Then drag the options into your predicted ranking (most popular first)
3
Exact rank match = +400. One off = +200. Two off = +75
Pro Tip
Think about who's in the room, not what you personally prefer. You're guessing the group, not yourself.
Mafia 4+
10–20 min
›
A classic social deduction game. Most players are Villagers — but hidden among them are Mafia members. Special roles include Doctor (protect someone) and Detective (investigate someone). Village must find and vote out the Mafia before being outnumbered!
1
Everyone gets a secret role on their phone — don't show anyone!
2
At night: Mafia picks who to eliminate. Doctor protects someone. Detective investigates.
3
At day: discuss who you think the Mafia is, then everyone votes to eliminate a suspect
4
Mafia wins if they outnumber villagers. Village wins if all Mafia are eliminated
Pro Tip
Mafia: blend in and create doubt. Villagers: watch for who avoids accusations or deflects too quickly.
